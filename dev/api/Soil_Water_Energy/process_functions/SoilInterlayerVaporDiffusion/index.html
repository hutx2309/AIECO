Soil Interlayer Vapor Diffusion

Source: src/Soil_Water_Energy/process_functions/SoilInterlayerVaporDiffusion.jl

Process-level function for vapor diffusion exchange between soil layers.

API

Main.ScriptDocs.SoilInterlayerVaporDiffusion.soil_interlayer_vapor_diffusion_process — Method
soil_interlayer_vapor_diffusion_process(...)

Calculate vapor diffusion between adjacent soil micropore air domains.

# VAPOR PRESSURE AND DIFFUSIVITY IN EACH GRID CELL

The process is active only when both source and destination micropore air volumes exceed a small numerical gate:

\[V_{a,1} > \epsilon_a \quad \text{and} \quad V_{a,2} > \epsilon_a\]

where $V_a$ is the substep micropore air volume used by the vapor routine.

The vapor concentrations are

\[C_{v,1} = \max\left(0, \frac{V_{v,1}}{V_{a,1}}\right)\]

and

\[C_{v,2} = \max\left(0, \frac{V_{v,2}}{V_{a,2}}\right)\]

where $V_v$ is vapor storage and $V_a$ is air volume.

The effective vapor diffusivities are

\[D_{v,1} = D_{g,1} V_{a,1}^{diff} q_p \frac{V_{a,1}^{diff}}{\phi_1}\]

and

\[D_{v,2} = D_{g,2} V_{a,2}^{diff} q_p \frac{V_{a,2}^{diff}}{\phi_2}\]

where $D_g$ is the gas diffusivity coefficient, $V_a^{diff}$ is the air-volume term used for diffusivity, $q_p$ is the legacy porosity/tortuosity coefficient, and $\phi$ is porosity.

The interfacial vapor conductance is

\[D_{v,12} = \frac{ 2 D_{v,1} D_{v,2} }{ D_{v,1} L_2 + D_{v,2} L_1 }\]

The potential diffusive vapor flux is

\[F_v^* = D_{v,12} A (C_{v,1} - C_{v,2}) \Delta t\]

The mixed equilibrium vapor concentration is

\[C_{v,y} = \frac{ C_{v,1} V_{a,1} + C_{v,2} V_{a,2} }{ V_{a,1} + V_{a,2} }\]

and the storage-limited source-side vapor exchange is

\[F_{v,x} = (C_{v,1} - C_{v,y}) V_{a,1} f_r\]

The final vapor flux is

\[F_v = \begin{cases} \max(0, \min(F_v^*, F_{v,x})), & F_v^* \ge 0 \\ \min(0, \max(F_v^*, F_{v,x})), & F_v^* < 0 \end{cases}\]

The advective heat carried by vapor movement is

\[H_v = \begin{cases} c_w T_1 F_v, & F_v > 0 \\ c_w T_2 F_v, & F_v < 0 \\ 0, & F_v = 0 \end{cases}\]

implemented by

advective_heat_by_vapor_flux(F_v, T_1, T_2, cpw)

Sign convention

  • vapor_flux > 0: vapor moves from source layer to destination layer.
  • vapor_flux < 0: vapor moves from destination layer to source layer.

Returns

A named tuple with activation status, vapor concentrations, vapor diffusivities, interfacial conductance, potential vapor flux, limited vapor flux, and advective heat flux.
