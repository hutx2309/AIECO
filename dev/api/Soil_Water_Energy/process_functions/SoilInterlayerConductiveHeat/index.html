Soil Interlayer Conductive Heat

Source: src/Soil_Water_Energy/process_functions/SoilInterlayerConductiveHeat.jl

Process-level function for conductive heat transfer between soil layers.

API

Main.ScriptDocs.SoilInterlayerConductiveHeat.soil_interlayer_conductive_heat_process — Method
soil_interlayer_conductive_heat_process(...)

Calculate conductive heat exchange between adjacent soil cells.

# THERMAL CONDUCTIVITY IN EACH GRID CELL
# HEAT FLOW FROM THERMAL CONDUCTIVITY AND TEMPERATURE GRADIENT

The source and destination thermal conductivities are calculated from mineral, rock, water, ice, and air contributions. The dynamic water and air conductivity enhancement terms are controlled by the local temperature-gradient scale:

\[\Delta T_x = 10^{-6} |T_1 - T_2|\]

For each cell, the water and air enhancement factors are

\[R_w = \min\left(10^4, r_w \Delta T_x \max(0, \theta_w - \theta_{w,r})^3\right)\]

and

\[R_a = \min\left(10^4, r_a \Delta T_x \max(0, \theta_a - \theta_{a,r})^3\right)\]

with corresponding Nusselt-like factors

\[N_w = \max\left(1, 0.68 + \frac{0.67 R_w^{1/4}}{d_w}\right)\]

and

\[N_a = \max\left(1, 0.68 + \frac{0.67 R_a^{1/4}}{d_a}\right)\]

The effective cell thermal conductivity follows the legacy WF8 mixture form:

\[k = \frac{ k_m + \theta_w k_w + 0.611 \theta_i k_i + w_\theta \theta_a k_a }{ k_r + \theta_w + 0.611 \theta_i + w_\theta \theta_a }\]

where

\[w_\theta = 1.467 - 0.467 \phi_a\]

The interface conductance is calculated as a series conductance:

\[G_{12} = \frac{2 k_1 k_2}{k_1 L_2 + k_2 L_1}\]

Before conductive exchange is calculated, the temperatures are adjusted for vapor heat transport. For the source cell,

\[T_1' = T_1 - \frac{H_v}{C_1}\]

or, for the top soil layer without snow cover,

\[T_1' = T_1 - \frac{H_v - H_g}{C_1}\]

where $H_v$ is vapor advective heat and $H_g$ is ground heat exchange. The destination cell is adjusted as

\[T_2' = T_2 + \frac{H_v}{C_2}\]

The equilibrium temperature is

\[T_y = \frac{C_1 T_1' + C_2 T_2'}{C_1 + C_2}\]

The source-side sensible heat availability is

\[H_x = (T_1' - T_y) C_1 f_r\]

The potential conductive heat exchange is

\[H_c = G_{12} A (T_1' - T_2') \Delta t\]

The final limited conductive heat exchange is

\[H = \begin{cases} \max(0, \min(H_x, H_c)), & H_c \ge 0 \\ \min(0, \max(H_x, H_c)), & H_c < 0 \end{cases}\]

Sign convention

  • conductive_heat_flux > 0: heat moves from source to destination.
  • conductive_heat_flux < 0: heat moves from destination to source.

Returns

A named tuple with source/destination thermal conductivities, adjusted temperatures, equilibrium temperature, potential conductive heat, sensible heat limit, and final limited conductive heat.
