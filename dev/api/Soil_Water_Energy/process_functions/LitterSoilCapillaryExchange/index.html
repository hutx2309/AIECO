Litter-Soil Capillary Exchange

Source: src/Soil_Water_Energy/process_functions/LitterSoilCapillaryExchange.jl

Process-level function for capillary water exchange between surface litter and the upper soil micropore domain.

API

Main.ScriptDocs.LitterSoilCapillaryExchange.soil_litter_capillary_exchange_process — Method
soil_litter_capillary_exchange_process(...)

Calculate one substep of capillary water exchange between surface litter and the upper soil layer.

This process represents water movement across the litter–soil interface driven by total water-potential differences.

The bounded volumetric water contents are

\[\theta_l = \min\left[ \theta_{l,\max}, \max\left(0, \frac{W_l}{V_l}\right) \right]\]

and

\[\theta_s = \min\left[ \phi_s, \max\left(\theta_{s,\min}, \frac{W_s}{V_s}\right) \right]\]

where W_l and W_s are litter and soil water storages, V_l is dry litter volume, V_s is soil reference volume, \theta_{l,\max} is the litter saturation water content, and \phi_s is soil porosity.

The total water potentials are

\[\Psi_l = \psi_{m,l} + \psi_{g,l} + \psi_{o,l}\]

and

\[\Psi_s = \psi_{m,s} + \psi_{g,s} + \psi_{o,s}\]

The litter–soil interface conductance is

\[C_{ls} = \frac{2 K_l K_s}{K_l L_s + K_s L_l}\]

where K_l and K_s are litter and soil hydraulic conductivities, and L_l and L_s are effective flow lengths.

The potential capillary flux is

\[F^* = C_{ls} A f_w (\Psi_l - \Psi_s) \Delta t\]

where A is exchange area, f_w is the water-covered fraction, and \Delta t is the capillary exchange time factor.

When the potential flux is near zero, a deadband is applied:

\[F^*_0 = \begin{cases} 0, & |F^*| < \epsilon \\ F^*, & |F^*| \ge \epsilon \end{cases}\]

The air-entry correction is

\[F_z = \begin{cases} F^*_0 + \min\left[ (\theta_l - \theta_{ae,l}) V_l, \max(0, (\theta_{ae,s} - \theta_s) V_s) \right] f_r, & F^*_0 > 0,\ \theta_l > \theta_{ae,l} \\ F^*_0 + \max\left[ (\theta_{ae,s} - \theta_s) V_s, \min(0, (\theta_l - \theta_{ae,l}) V_l) \right] f_r, & F^*_0 < 0,\ \theta_s > \theta_{ae,s} \\ F^*_0, & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\]

The final flux is storage-limited:

\[F = \begin{cases} \min(F_z, W_l f_r, P_s), & F_z > 0 \\ \max(F_z, -W_s f_r, -P_l), & F_z < 0 \end{cases}\]

where positive flux means litter-to-soil flow, P_l is litter pore capacity, and P_s is soil pore capacity.

If the soil micropore domain has negative capacity, an additional correction is applied:

\[F \leftarrow F + \min\left[ 0, \max(-W_s f_r, P_{s,raw}) \right] \quad \text{if } P_{s,raw} < 0\]

The advective heat flux is

\[H_F = \begin{cases} c_w F T_l, & F > 0 \\ c_w F T_s, & F < 0 \end{cases}\]

implemented by

advective_heat_by_water_flux(F, T_l, T_s, cpw)

Sign convention

  • limited_flux > 0: water moves from surface litter to soil.
  • limited_flux < 0: water moves from soil to surface litter.

Returns

A named tuple containing water contents, potentials, conductance, potential flux, corrected flux, limited flux, heat flux, and updated substep storages.
