Surface Excess Overland Runoff

Source: src/Soil_Water_Energy/process_functions/Surf_ExcessOverlandRunoff.jl

Process-level function for excess overland runoff across the surface.

API

Main.ScriptDocs.Surf_ExcessOverlandRunoff.surface_excess_overland_runoff_process — Method
surface_excess_overland_runoff_process(...)

Partition excess surface litter water/ice above litter holding capacity and calculate overland runoff from free surface liquid water.

The candidate post-flux surface liquid water is

\[W_l' = W_l + F_{surf} + F_{evap} + F_{ft}\]

where $W_l$ is current litter liquid water, $F_{surf}$ is the net liquid flux into surface litter storage, $F_{evap}$ is vapor-liquid exchange, and $F_{ft}$ is freeze-thaw liquid-water-equivalent flux.

The candidate post-flux surface ice is

\[I_l' = I_l - \frac{F_{ft}}{\rho_i}\]

where $\rho_i$ is the ice-density conversion factor.

The total water plus ice above litter holding capacity is partitioned into free liquid and free ice using the legacy ratio-preserving rule:

\[E = \max(0, W_l' + I_l' - C_l)\]

\[W_f = \max\left(0, W_l' - \frac{W_l'}{W_l' + I_l'} C_l\right)\]

\[I_f = \max\left(0, I_l' - \frac{I_l'}{W_l' + I_l'} C_l\right)\]

where $C_l$ is litter water holding capacity.

Overland runoff occurs only when free surface liquid exceeds ground-water retention capacity:

\[W_x = W_f - W_r\]

If $W_x > 0$, the water depth and hydraulic radius are

\[D = \frac{W_x}{A_s}\]

and

\[R = \frac{D}{2.828}\]

The surface velocity is

\[v = \frac{ R^{0.67} \sqrt{\max(0, S)} }{ z_m }\]

and the potential runoff is

\[Q^* = v D A_f 3.6 \times 10^3 \Delta t\]

The final runoff is limited by excess free liquid and available liquid water:

\[Q = \min(Q^*, W_x f_r, W_{avail} f_r)\]

note

available_liquid_for_runoff = max(0.0, surfW_Micpore_L + watFlux_littFT)

rather than the full post-flux liquid storage. This function preserves that behavior by default.

Returns

A named tuple containing post-flux surface liquid/ice, total excess water/ice, free liquid, free ice, runoff, runoff velocity, and runoff-limiting diagnostics.
