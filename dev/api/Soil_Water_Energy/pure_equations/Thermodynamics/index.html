Thermodynamics

Source: src/Soil_Water_Energy/pure_equations/Thermodynamics.jl

Pure helpers for saturation vapor concentration, vapor equilibrium under water potential, latent and advective heat, material heat capacity, temperature from energy, equilibrium temperature, and conductive/sensible heat exchange.

These functions return values and do not mutate model state.

API

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.VP_at_psi — Method
VP_at_psi(TK, ψ)

Calculate equilibrium vapor concentration near a water/soil/litter surface, reduced by water potential.

Equation:

\[VP(T, \psi) = VP_{sat}(T)\,\exp\left(\frac{18\psi}{RT}\right)\]

Inputs: TK : temperature [K] ψ : water potential [MPa] R : gas constant [8.3143 J/(mol*K)] 18: J/mol/MPa conversion factor Output: vapor concentration (m3 water-equivalent vapor /m3 air = g/m3)

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.advective_heat_vapor — Method
advective_heat_vapor(vapor_flux, TK, cpw)

Heat carried by moving vapor flux.

Inputs: vapor_flux : vapor water-equivalent volume/depth for current substep TK : temperature [K] cpw : water-equivalent heat capacity [MJ m⁻³ K⁻¹]

Output: heat carried by vapor flux [MJ] or [MJ m⁻²], consistent with vapor_flux

Note: This is not latent heat of evaporation. It is the sensible/enthalpy content transported by vapor movement.

advective_heat_water(water_flux, TK, cpw)

Heat carried by liquid water flux.

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.conductive_heat_flux — Method
conductive_heat_flux(conductance, T_source, T_dest)

Simple temperature-gradient heat flux.

\[H = G(T_{source} - T_{dest})\]

Positive value means heat moves from source to destination if T_source > T_dest.

source
Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.equilibrium_temperature — Method
equilibrium_temperature(C1, T1, C2, T2)

Energy-weighted equilibrium temperature between two connected stores.

\[T_{eq} = \frac{C_1T_1 + C_2T_2}{C_1 + C_2}\]

source
Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.heat_capacity_litter — Method
heat_capacity_litter(organic_mass, water, vapor, ice; cpo, cpw, cpi)

Surface litter/residue heat capacity.

\[C_{litter} = c_{po}M_o + c_{pw}(W + V) + c_{pi}I\]

organic_mass can be surfSOC + surfcharcoal or another dry organic pool.

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.heat_capacity_snow — Method
heat_capacity_snow(snow, water, ice, vapor; cps, cpw, cpi)

Snowpack heat capacity from snow, liquid water, ice, and vapor.

\[C_{snow} = c_{ps}S + c_{pw}(W + V) + c_{pi}I\]

source
Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.heat_capacity_soil — Method
heat_capacity_soil(dry_heat_capacity, water, vapor, ice, water_macro, ice_macro; cpw, cpi)

Soil heat capacity including dry soil, micropore water/vapor/ice, and macropore water/ice.

\[C_{soil} = C_{dry} + c_{pw}(W_\mu + V_\mu + W_M) + c_{pi}(I_\mu + I_M)\]

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.latent_heat_evaporation — Method
latent_heat_evaporation(water_flux, latent_heat)

Convert evaporation/condensation water flux to latent heat flux. Equation:

\[H_{latent} = W L_v\]

Inputs: W: evaporation (positive) or condensation (negative) flux [m3] Lv: latent heat of vaporization [MJ/m3]

Positive or negative sign follows the sign of water_flux.

source
Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.sensible_heat_limited — Method
sensible_heat_limited(T_source, T_equil, heat_capacity, substep_fraction)

Maximum sensible heat exchange needed to move source toward equilibrium.

\[H_{sens} = (T_{source} - T_{eq}) C\, f_{step}\]

Main.ScriptDocs.Thermodynamics.temperature_from_energy — Method
temperature_from_energy(old_heat_capacity, old_temp, net_heat, new_heat_capacity, fallback_temp, min_heat_capacity)

Update temperature from energy balance.

Equation:

\[T_{new} = \frac{C_{old}T_{old} + H_{net}}{C_{new}}\]

If new heat capacity is too small, return fallback temperature.
